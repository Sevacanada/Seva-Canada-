01

What We Stand For

These are the non-negotiable beliefs that drive every program, post, and partnership. Everything the campaign does must be rooted in at least one of these.

B-01

We embrace Canadian values — fully, not selectively

Equality, inclusion, multiculturalism, rule of law, freedom of expression — these are not compromises of our identity; they are extensions of it. Indians who thrive here understand that belonging is a two-way commitment.

B-02

We give back without keeping score

Seva — selfless service — is the oldest Indian value there is. We serve food banks, clean parks, mentor youth, and support local causes because it's the right thing to do. Not for visibility. Not for credit.

B-03

We help everyone — no ethnic filter

If we only serve the Indian community, we confirm that we keep to ourselves. True integration means standing in line to help a First Nations elder, a Ukrainian refugee, or a single mother in Salmon Arm.

B-04

We amplify Indian-Canadians doing good — loudly

For every negative headline about Indians in Canada, there are fifty stories of generosity, innovation, and leadership that go untold. We become the counter-narrative press team.

B-05

We collaborate with any good cause, any people

Indigenous reconciliation, LGBTQ+ inclusion, food security, environmental work — if the cause is good and the intent is genuine, we show up. Alliances are how movements grow.

02

Canadian Values — The Bridge We Build On

Indians have deep cultural roots. Canada has deep civic roots. This campaign shows they are not in conflict — they are complementary. Here is how we teach that bridge within the Indian community.

Canadian Values We Champion

🍁
Multiculturalism
Canada was built on difference. We celebrate our heritage while celebrating others' too.
⚖️
Equality & Human Rights
We stand against discrimination — including casteism, colourism, and patriarchy within our own community.
🤲
Community Responsibility
Active participation in civic life — voting, volunteering, mentoring — is part of being Canadian.
🌿
Environmental Stewardship
Canadians care deeply about nature. Indians must too — and Indian philosophy of harmony with earth supports this.
🏛️
Indigenous Reconciliation
As settlers on this land, Indians have a responsibility to understand and support First Nations communities.

How We Teach These Inside the Community

📚
New Arrival Orientation
"Welcome to Canada" workshops for new Indian immigrants — cultural norms, civic rights, and responsibilities.
💬
Open Conversations
Safe, honest discussions within the community about casteism, gender discrimination, and integration challenges.
🎙️
Speaker Series
Indian-Canadians who model civic engagement sharing how they blend heritage with Canadian identity.
🏫
Youth Education
Work with schools to educate Indian-Canadian youth on history, reconciliation, and civic leadership.
📰
Social Media Literacy
Teach Indians to stop sharing divisive content — to be critical consumers and responsible digital citizens.
03

Signature Social Programs

These are the tangible, ground-level initiatives the campaign runs. Each program is designed to create visible, real impact — not just awareness.

🫙
Flagship Program

Seva Table

Monthly free community meals open to everyone — no membership, no Indian-only tables. Partner with local food banks, churches, and mosques. Let food do the unifying.

  • Monthly community dinners in Vernon/Kelowna
  • Partner with existing food banks and shelters
  • Feature different Indian regional cuisines each time
  • Invite non-Indian co-hosts every session
🌱
Environment Program

Green Hands

Indians leading environmental action in the Okanagan. Tree planting, trail cleanups, and partnerships with BC environmental organizations. Connects seva with Canadian love of nature.

  • Quarterly Okanagan cleanup drives
  • Tree planting on Diwali — "A tree for every lamp lit"
  • Partner with Okanagan Watershed Stewardship Council
  • Document and share impact publicly
🎓
Youth Program

Roots & Routes

Mentorship for Indian-Canadian youth — connecting them to Indian heritage AND Canadian opportunity. Workshops, career mentors, and leadership training.

  • Monthly mentorship circles (university & high school)
  • Resume, interview, and professional skills workshops
  • Pair Indian youth with First Nations youth for exchange
  • Annual "Young Indian-Canadian of the Year" award
🤝
Reconciliation Program

Two Roots, One Land

A deliberate program to build relationships between Indian-Canadians and Indigenous communities. Learn the land's true history — and contribute to healing it.

  • Host Indigenous speakers at Indian community events
  • Participate in National Day for Truth and Reconciliation
  • Support Indigenous-owned businesses publicly
  • Land acknowledgement education within Indian community
04

The Narrative We're Building

Right now the story being told about Indians in Canada is shaped by headlines. We change that by flooding the zone with real stories of service, integration, and leadership.

The Narrative We're Replacing
  • Indians keep to themselves
  • Indians only look out for each other
  • Indians bring conflict from back home
  • Indians don't integrate or adapt
  • Indians are just economic migrants
The Narrative We're Building
  • Indians are pillars of their communities
  • Indians serve everyone without discrimination
  • Indians model Canada's multicultural promise
  • Indians honour Canadian values while celebrating heritage
  • Indians are invested Canadians building this country's future

06

12-Month Execution Plan

Three phases. Each builds on the last. The goal is to be visibly serving the community within 90 days of launch.

Months 1–3
Lay
the
Ground
Build the core, establish identity, run the first Seva Table

Before going public, assemble a founding team of 8–12 Indian-Canadians from diverse regional and religious backgrounds. Lock in campaign name, visual identity, social channels, and a simple one-page manifesto. Then run your first community meal within 90 days — action first, content second.

  • Recruit founding team — specifically diverse: Punjabi, Malayali, Gujarati, Tamil, Bengali
  • Register as a society or non-profit (BC Societies Act) for credibility and funding eligibility
  • Launch Instagram + Facebook + YouTube with 10 foundational posts
  • Host first Seva Table community meal — open to all, partner with one non-Indian org
  • Reach out to Okanagan Nation Alliance to begin reconciliation conversation
Months 4–7
Build
Momentum
Programs running, content flowing, partnerships forming

Now the engine is running. Seva Table is monthly. Green Hands runs its first cleanup. Content tells real stories. Media starts paying attention.

  • Launch "Indians Doing Good" — weekly social media spotlight on Indian-Canadians in service
  • First Green Hands cleanup drive — target Okanagan Lake shoreline or a local trail
  • Host "What Does It Mean to Be Indian-Canadian?" — community panel open to all
  • Approach Vernon Morning Star and CBC Okanagan with a story pitch
  • Launch new arrival orientation workshop — partner with settlement services
  • Begin Roots & Routes mentorship with Okanagan College students
Months 8–12
Become
a
Force
A major public event, media presence, and a replicable model

By year end, Seva Canada is a recognized name in the Okanagan. Other cities are asking how to replicate it. The Indian community's reputation is visibly shifting.

  • Host the first Seva Canada Day — public festival of service, food, culture, and community
  • Announce first Seva Canada Community Award — recognizing non-Indian ally of the year
  • Publish impact report: meals served, trees planted, hours volunteered, partnerships formed
  • Pitch campaign model to Indian community associations in Kelowna, Kamloops, and Penticton
  • Begin Year 2 planning with double the programs and a grant application to Canadian Heritage
